# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our
community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall
  community
* Using professional language and maintaining courtesy in all interactions
* Respecting project management methodologies and diverse approaches
* Sharing knowledge and helping others learn and grow

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of
  any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address,
  without their explicit permission
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting
* Deliberately undermining project management practices or methodologies
* Sharing templates containing sensitive or inappropriate content
* Violating intellectual property rights or licensing terms

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.
Examples of representing our community include using an official e-mail address,
posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event.

This includes:

* GitHub repository interactions (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Community forums and discussion platforms
* Social media interactions related to the project
* Conference presentations and workshops
* Online and offline events and meetups
* Private communications in the context of the project

## Project-Specific Guidelines

### Template Contributions

* **Quality Standards**: Ensure all template contributions meet our quality guidelines
* **No Sensitive Data**: Never include real credentials, personal information, or proprietary data
* **Professional Content**: All content should be appropriate for professional environments
* **Methodology Respect**: Respect different project management approaches and methodologies
* **Documentation**: Provide clear documentation and usage instructions

### Community Interactions

* **Constructive Feedback**: Provide helpful, specific, and actionable feedback
* **Knowledge Sharing**: Share experiences and learnings generously
* **Inclusive Language**: Use language that welcomes all skill levels and backgrounds
* **Problem-Solving Focus**: Keep discussions focused on solutions and improvements
* **Recognition**: Acknowledge the contributions and expertise of others

### Professional Standards

* **Industry Best Practices**: Follow recognized project management standards and practices
* **Continuous Learning**: Stay open to new ideas and methodologies
* **Ethical Behavior**: Maintain the highest standards of professional ethics
* **Confidentiality**: Respect confidential information and proprietary methods
* **Accuracy**: Strive for accuracy in all templates and documentation

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at
[conduct@pm-tools-templates.org] or through GitHub's reporting mechanisms.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Reporting Guidelines

When reporting issues, please include:

* **Description**: Clear description of the incident
* **Evidence**: Links, screenshots, or other supporting evidence
* **Impact**: How the behavior affected you or the community
* **Context**: Relevant background information
* **Desired Outcome**: What resolution you're seeking

### Privacy and Confidentiality

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident. Reports will be handled confidentially, and reporter
identity will be protected unless disclosure is required for resolution.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of
actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent
ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the
community.

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

Individuals subject to enforcement actions have the right to appeal decisions
through the following process:

1. **Initial Appeal**: Submit appeal within 30 days to [appeals@pm-tools-templates.org]
2. **Review Process**: Independent review by uninvolved community leaders
3. **Decision Timeline**: Response within 14 days of appeal submission
4. **Final Appeal**: Option for final review by project leadership if needed

### Appeal Guidelines

Appeals should include:

* **Original Incident**: Reference to the original enforcement action
* **New Information**: Any new information relevant to the case
* **Changed Circumstances**: Evidence of changed behavior or understanding
* **Specific Request**: Clear statement of desired outcome

## Diversity and Inclusion

### Our Commitment

We are committed to creating a diverse and inclusive community that:

* **Welcomes All Backgrounds**: People from all industries, experience levels, and methodologies
* **Values Different Perspectives**: Recognizing that diversity strengthens our community
* **Provides Equal Opportunities**: Ensuring fair access to participation and leadership
* **Promotes Learning**: Creating an environment where everyone can grow and contribute

### Inclusive Practices

* **Accessible Content**: Ensuring templates and documentation are accessible to all users
* **Multiple Methodologies**: Supporting various project management approaches
* **Language Considerations**: Being mindful of non-native English speakers
* **Cultural Sensitivity**: Respecting different cultural approaches to project management
* **Skill Level Inclusion**: Welcoming both beginners and experts

## Updates and Revisions

* **Reflect Community Growth**: Adapt to changing community needs

* **Align with Best Practices**: Following evolving industry standards
* **Address New Challenges**: Responding to emerging issues
