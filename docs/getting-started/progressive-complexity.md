## Decision Framework: Which Level to Choose?

### Quick Assessment

**Answer these questions to determine your complexity level:**

#### Project Characteristics
1. **Team Size**
   - <5 people â†’ Level 1
   - 5-20 people â†’ Level 2
   - >20 people â†’ Level 3

2. **Budget**
   - <$25K â†’ Level 1
   - $25K-$500K â†’ Level 2
   - >$500K â†’ Level 3

3. **Duration**
   - <3 months â†’ Level 1
   - 3-12 months â†’ Level 2
   - >12 months â†’ Level 3

4. **Stakeholder Count**
   - <5 stakeholders â†’ Level 1
   - 5-15 stakeholders â†’ Level 2
   - >15 stakeholders â†’ Level 3

#### Organizational Factors
1. **Compliance Requirements**
   - None/minimal â†’ Level 1
   - Some industry standards â†’ Level 2
   - Heavy regulation â†’ Level 3

2. **Documentation Standards**
   - Informal â†’ Level 1
   - Professional â†’ Level 2
   - Enterprise/audit-ready â†’ Level 3

3. **Risk Level**
   - Low â†’ Level 1
   - Medium â†’ Level 2
   - High â†’ Level 3

### Scoring
**Count your answers:**
- Mostly Level 1: Start with Beginner templates
- Mostly Level 2: Use Intermediate templates
- Mostly Level 3: Choose Advanced templates
- Mixed results: Start one level lower and upgrade as needed

## Upgrade Guidelines

### When to Move to the Next Level

**From Level 1 to Level 2:**
- Team has grown beyond 5 people
- Stakeholders request more detailed reporting
- Project becomes business-critical
- Budget exceeds $25K
- External stakeholders get involved

**From Level 2 to Level 3:**
- Multiple projects need coordination
- Regulatory compliance required
- Board-level visibility needed
- Budget exceeds $500K
- Organizational standards demand it

### Upgrade Process
1. **Assess current template effectiveness**
2. **Identify specific gaps or requirements**
3. **Review next level template**
4. **Migrate existing content**
5. **Enhance with additional detail**
6. **Validate with stakeholders**

### Downgrade Guidelines

**When to Simplify:**
- Template takes too long to complete
- Stakeholders find it overwhelming
- Information is not being used
- Project complexity decreased
- Team feedback suggests overkill

## Common Progression Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Growing Startup
**Months 1-6:** Level 1 templates for rapid iteration  
**Months 6-18:** Level 2 as processes mature  
**Months 18+:** Level 3 for investor/compliance requirements  

### Scenario 2: New PM in Large Organization
**Week 1-4:** Level 1 to build confidence  
**Month 2-6:** Level 2 for stakeholder credibility  
**Month 6+:** Level 3 to meet organizational standards  

### Scenario 3: Agile Transformation
**Phase 1:** Level 1 for quick wins and adoption  
**Phase 2:** Level 2 for sustainable practices  
**Phase 3:** Level 3 for organizational scaling
