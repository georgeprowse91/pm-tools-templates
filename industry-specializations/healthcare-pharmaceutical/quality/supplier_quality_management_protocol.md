## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This Supplier Quality Management Protocol establishes a standardized framework for the evaluation, qualification, management, and monitoring of suppliers that provide materials, components, products, and services that directly or indirectly impact product quality, safety, and efficacy in accordance with cGMP (current Good Manufacturing Practices), ISO 13485, ICH Q10, and other applicable regulatory requirements.

### 1.2 Scope

This protocol applies to all suppliers providing:
- Raw materials and APIs (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients)
- Excipients and packaging components
- Production equipment and instrumentation
- Laboratory equipment, consumables, and reference standards
- Contract manufacturing, laboratory, and research services
- Validation, calibration, and maintenance services
- Software and computerized systems used in GxP applications
- Consultancy services related to quality and regulatory compliance

### 1.3 Regulatory Framework

This protocol aligns with requirements set forth in:
- FDA 21 CFR Parts 210/211 (cGMP for Finished Pharmaceuticals)
- FDA 21 CFR Part 820 (Quality System Regulation for Medical Devices)
- EU GMP Guidelines (EudraLex Volume 4)
- ICH Q7 (Good Manufacturing Practice for APIs)
- ICH Q9 (Quality Risk Management)
- ICH Q10 (Pharmaceutical Quality System)
- ISO 9001:2015 (Quality Management Systems Requirements)
- ISO 13485:2016 (Medical Devices - Quality Management Systems)

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

### 2.1 Quality Unit

- Provides final approval for supplier qualification decisions
- Oversees the implementation of this protocol
- Evaluates supplier quality systems and approves quality agreements
- Participates in supplier audits
- Reviews supplier quality metrics and performance data
- Approves corrective and preventive actions for supplier quality issues

### 2.2 Procurement Department

- Initiates supplier identification process
- Coordinates commercial agreements with suppliers
- Maintains supplier database with current contact information
- Communicates commercial requirements to suppliers
- Collaborates with Quality Unit on supplier management activities

### 2.3 Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)

- Provide technical input for supplier selection and qualification
- Define technical and quality requirements for supplied materials/services
- Participate in technical assessments and qualification activities
- Support investigation of supplier-related quality issues

### 2.4 Supplier Quality Management Team

- Coordinates supplier qualification activities
- Maintains supplier qualification documentation
- Schedules and conducts supplier audits
- Monitors supplier performance
- Manages supplier corrective action requests
- Maintains the approved supplier list

## 3. Supplier Classification System

### 3.1 Risk-Based Classification Criteria

Suppliers shall be classified based on the following risk assessment criteria:

| Risk Level | Description | Examples |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| **Critical** | Direct impact on product quality, safety, or efficacy. No or limited incoming inspection possible. | APIs, sterile materials, contract manufacturers, sterilization services |
| **Major** | Significant potential impact on product quality or GMP compliance. Limited testing available. | Excipients, primary packaging materials, analytical testing services |
| **Minor** | Indirect impact on product quality. Extensive testing or verification possible. | Secondary packaging, calibration services, general laboratory supplies |
| **Standard** | No direct impact on product quality, but may impact GMP operations. | Office supplies, consulting services, general maintenance |

### 3.2 Classification Matrix

The final supplier classification combines the assessment of:
- Material/service criticality (impact on product)
- Complexity of manufacturing/service process
- Regulatory compliance requirements
- Availability of alternative suppliers
- Historical performance (if applicable)

### 3.3 Qualification Requirements by Classification

| Classification | Initial Assessment | On-site Audit | Quality Agreement | Periodic Re-evaluation | Performance Monitoring |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|-------------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Critical | Comprehensive | Mandatory | Detailed | Annual | Quarterly |
| Major | Comprehensive | Required | Standard | Every 2 years | Semi-annual |
| Minor | Basic | As needed | Simplified | Every 3 years | Annual |
| Standard | Minimal | Not required | Not required | As needed | As needed |

## 4. Supplier Qualification Process

### 4.1 Initial Supplier Assessment

#### 4.1.1 Initial Screening
- Business stability and financial evaluation
- Regulatory compliance history and certification status
- Geographic location risk assessment
- Preliminary capability assessment
- Conflict of interest check

#### 4.1.2 Supplier Questionnaire
A standardized questionnaire shall be sent to potential suppliers covering:
- Quality management system details
- Regulatory certifications and compliance history
- Manufacturing/service capabilities
- Change control and notification processes
- Supply chain management
- Complaint handling and recall procedures
- Business continuity planning

#### 4.1.3 Document Review
Evaluation of supplier documentation, which may include:
- Quality manual and relevant procedures
- Certifications (ISO, industry-specific)
- Manufacturing/service process flow and controls
- Qualification/validation approach
- Recent regulatory inspection reports
- Stability data (if applicable)
- Master batch records (if applicable)

### 4.2 Technical Capability Assessment

#### 4.2.1 Technical Requirements
Detailed specification of requirements including:
- Material/service specifications
- Manufacturing/process requirements
- Testing and release criteria
- Packaging and labeling requirements
- Transportation and storage conditions
- Documentation requirements

#### 4.2.2 Capability Evaluation
Assessment of supplier's ability to consistently meet requirements:
- Technical resources and expertise
- Equipment and technology assessment
- Process controls and monitoring systems
- Testing capabilities and methodologies
- Resource capacity and flexibility

#### 4.2.3 Sample Evaluation (where applicable)
- Evaluation of representative samples or demonstrations
- Laboratory testing against specifications
- Pilot-scale manufacturing trial (if required)
- Process validation assessment

### 4.3 Quality System Assessment

#### 4.3.1 On-site Audit (for Critical and Major suppliers)
Comprehensive evaluation of supplier's quality management system covering:
- Quality management and organization
- Personnel qualifications and training
- Documentation and records management
- Change control processes
- Validation activities
- Production and process controls
- Laboratory controls and testing
- Material management
- Equipment qualification and maintenance
- Contamination and cross-contamination controls
- Deviation management
- Complaint handling
- Corrective and preventive action (CAPA) system
- Supplier management (sub-suppliers)
- Data integrity controls

#### 4.3.2 Remote Assessment (when on-site audit is not feasible)
- Virtual facility tour
- Remote document review
- Video interviews with key personnel
- Review of third-party audit reports

#### 4.3.3 Audit Report and Response
- Documented findings and observations
- Classification of findings (critical, major, minor)
- Corrective action requests
- Supplier response and corrective action plan review
- Verification of effectiveness of implemented actions

### 4.4 Risk Assessment

A formal risk assessment shall be conducted for each supplier using:
- FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis) methodology
- Risk priority numbers based on severity, occurrence, and detection
- Mitigation strategies for identified risks
- Residual risk evaluation

### 4.5 Approval Process

#### 4.5.1 Qualification Decision
Based on the cumulative evaluation of:
- Initial assessment results
- Technical capability assessment
- Quality system assessment
- Risk assessment results
- Response to corrective actions

#### 4.5.2 Qualification Statuses
- **Approved**: Meets all requirements without significant concerns
- **Conditionally Approved**: Meets critical requirements but has minor deficiencies requiring correction
- **Not Approved**: Fails to meet critical requirements or has significant deficiencies

#### 4.5.3 Approved Supplier List
Maintenance of an Approved Supplier List (ASL) containing:
- Supplier name and contact information
- Approved materials/services
- Qualification status and date
- Classification level
- Re-evaluation due date
- Key qualification documents reference

## 5. Supplier Quality Agreements

### 5.1 Quality Agreement Requirements

Quality Agreements shall be established for all Critical and Major suppliers, covering:

#### 5.1.1 General Information
- Scope and purpose
- Effective date and validity period
- Contact information for key personnel
- Confidentiality provisions
- Definitions of terms

#### 5.1.2 Quality Responsibilities
Clear delineation of responsibilities between both parties for:
- Compliance with regulatory requirements
- Quality control testing and specifications
- Release procedures and certification
- Change management and notification
- Deviation handling
- Complaint investigation
- Recall procedures
- Stability testing (if applicable)
- Validation activities
- Record retention

#### 5.1.3 Communication Requirements
- Regulatory inspections notification
- Product quality issues notification
- Change control notification timeframes
- Regular quality meetings schedule
- Escalation procedures

#### 5.1.4 Auditing Rights
- Frequency and scope of routine audits
- Notification period for routine audits
- Provisions for for-cause audits
- Access to facilities and documentation

#### 5.1.5 Sub-supplier Management
- Notification requirements for sub-suppliers
- Approval process for critical sub-suppliers
- Responsibilities for sub-supplier qualification

### 5.2 Quality Agreement Review and Approval

- Legal review of agreement terms
- Quality review and approval
- Periodic review of agreement (minimum every 3 years)
- Change management for agreement updates

## 6. Ongoing Monitoring and Performance Metrics

### 6.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

#### 6.1.1 Quality Metrics
- Lot acceptance rate
- Out-of-specification (OOS) rate
- Deviation frequency
- CAPA effectiveness
- Change control compliance
- Documentation accuracy

#### 6.1.2 Delivery Metrics
- On-time delivery performance
- Complete shipment rate
- Documentation completeness

#### 6.1.3 Service Metrics
- Responsiveness to inquiries
- Investigation timeliness
- CAPA implementation timeliness
- Technical support quality

### 6.2 Scorecard System

Implementation of supplier scorecards that:
- Provide quantitative assessment of performance
- Allow for trend analysis over time
- Facilitate comparison between similar suppliers
- Support data-driven decisions

### 6.3 Performance Review Meetings

- Frequency based on supplier classification
- Standard agenda and performance data review
- Documentation of meeting outcomes
- Action item tracking

### 6.4 Continuous Improvement Initiatives

- Joint improvement projects
- Knowledge sharing
- Process optimization activities
- Technology upgrades

## 7. Supplier Audits

### 7.1 Audit Program

#### 7.1.1 Audit Types
- **Initial Qualification Audit**: Before approving a new supplier
- **Routine Surveillance Audit**: Periodic verification of compliance
- **For-Cause Audit**: Triggered by quality issues or significant changes
- **Re-qualification Audit**: Comprehensive assessment for periodic re-approval

#### 7.1.2 Audit Scheduling
- Risk-based frequency determination
- Annual audit program
- Coordination with supplier
- Resource allocation

### 7.2 Audit Preparation

- Audit team selection
- Audit plan development
- Pre-audit questionnaire
- Document request list
- Previous findings review

### 7.3 Audit Execution

- Opening meeting
- Facility tour
- Document review
- Process observation
- Personnel interviews
- Finding classification
- Closing meeting presentation

### 7.4 Audit Documentation

- Detailed audit report
- Finding categorization (critical, major, minor)
- Supporting evidence
- Recommendation for qualification status

### 7.5 Audit Follow-up

- Corrective action request issuance
- Review of supplier CAPA plans
- Verification of CAPA implementation
- Effectiveness checks
- Audit closure documentation

---

## 8. Change Control and Deviation Management

### 8.1 Supplier Change Notification Requirements

Suppliers must notify in advance of changes to:
- Manufacturing location or facility layout
- Raw material sources
- Manufacturing process or equipment
- Test methods or specifications
- Key personnel
- Quality management system
- Computer systems affecting product quality
- Sub-suppliers for critical components

### 8.2 Change Impact Assessment

- Risk assessment of proposed changes
- Determination of necessary verification activities
- Regulatory impact evaluation
- Implementation strategy and timeline

### 8.3 Deviation Management

#### 8.3.1 Deviation Notification Requirements
- Timeframe for notification
- Required information
- Initial containment actions

#### 8.3.2 Deviation Investigation
- Root cause analysis methodology
- Investigation timeline expectations
- Documentation requirements

#### 8.3.3 Impact Assessment
- Product quality impact evaluation
- Patient safety considerations
- Regulatory reporting requirements

### 8.4 Nonconforming Materials Management

- Quarantine procedures
- Investigation requirements
- Disposition decision-making process
- Rework/reprocessing controls
- Destruction documentation

---

## 9. Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) System

### 9.1 CAPA Initiation Criteria

- Quality agreement violations
- Repeated quality issues
- Critical or major audit findings
- Significant deviations
- OOS results or trends
- Customer complaints related to supplier

### 9.2 CAPA Request Process

- Standardized CAPA request format
- Required elements (description, containment, investigation)
- Response timeframe expectations
- Escalation process for delayed responses

### 9.3 CAPA Plan Evaluation

- Appropriateness of root cause analysis
- Comprehensiveness of corrective actions
- Preventive action adequacy
- Implementation timeline reasonableness
- Effectiveness monitoring plan

### 9.4 CAPA Verification and Closure

- Evidence of implementation review
- Effectiveness assessment methodology
- Verification of sustained improvement
- Formal closure process
- Lessons learned documentation

---

## 10. Supplier Disqualification and Exit Strategy

### 10.1 Disqualification Criteria

- Critical non-compliances with regulatory requirements
- Persistent quality issues without effective remediation
- Significant breach of quality agreement
- Loss of necessary certifications or licenses
- Falsification of data or fraud
- Financial instability affecting quality
- Unacceptable audit findings without adequate response

### 10.2 Disqualification Process

- Warning notification and escalation process
- Final evaluation meeting
- Formal disqualification notification
- Removal from Approved Supplier List
- Regulatory notification (if required)

### 10.3 Exit Strategy

- Inventory management plan
- Knowledge transfer requirements
- Alternative supplier qualification expediting
- Last-time buy considerations
- Document and sample retention requirements

---

## 11. Documentation and Record Keeping

### 11.1 Required Documentation

- Supplier qualification records
- Technical assessments
- Audit reports and CAPAs
- Quality agreements
- Specifications and requirements
- Performance data and trend analysis
- Change notifications and approvals
- Deviation reports and investigations
- Certificate of Analysis/Conformance
- Regulatory certifications
- Correspondence regarding quality issues

### 11.2 Document Control

- Secure storage and access controls
- Retention periods (minimum 7 years or per regulations)
- Retrieval system
- Archiving procedures
- Electronic record controls

### 11.3 Supplier Master File

Maintenance of comprehensive supplier files containing:
- Contact information
- Qualification status and history
- Product/service approval information
- Quality agreement
- Audit history
- Performance metrics
- Significant quality events
- Change history

## 12. Training Requirements

### 12.1 Internal Training

- Training on this protocol for all relevant personnel
- Supplier audit training for auditors
- Technical training for SMEs involved in qualification
- CAPA management training
- Change control evaluation training

### 12.2 Supplier Training

- Communication of quality requirements
- Change notification procedures training
- Quality agreement compliance training
- Specific technical requirements training

## 13. Protocol Review and Revision

This protocol shall be reviewed at minimum every two years or when significant regulatory changes occur. The review shall consider:
- Regulatory updates
- Organizational changes
- Process improvements
- Lessons learned from implementation
- Industry best practices
