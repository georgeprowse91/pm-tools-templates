## 9. Deviation Management

### 9.1 Deviation Handling Procedure
Any deviations observed during validation will be documented, investigated, and resolved according to the following procedure:
1. Document the deviation on the test script where it occurred
2. Complete a Deviation Form (attached as Appendix A)
3. Classify the deviation as critical, major, or minor
4. Investigate the root cause of the deviation
5. Determine the impact on validation activities and product quality
6. Develop and implement corrective and preventive actions (CAPA)
7. Obtain appropriate approvals for the CAPA plan
8. Implement the approved CAPA plan
9. Verify effectiveness of the implemented actions
10. Close the deviation with final approval

### 9.2 Deviation Classification
Deviations will be classified according to the following criteria:
- **Critical:** Direct impact on product quality, safety, or efficacy; regulatory non-compliance
- **Major:** Significant impact on validation activities; potential indirect impact on product quality
- **Minor:** No impact on product quality; minimal impact on validation activities

### 9.3 Retest Requirements
Retest requirements will be determined based on the nature and classification of the deviation:
- Critical deviations: Complete revalidation may be required
- Major deviations: Partial revalidation may be required
- Minor deviations: Specific test rerun may be sufficient

## 10. Documentation Requirements

### 10.1 Validation Documents
The following validation documents will be generated and maintained:
- Validation Protocol (this document)
- Test Scripts and Results
- Deviation Reports and CAPAs
- Raw Data and Test Records
- Final Validation Report
- Supporting Documentation (certificates, calibration records, etc.)

### 10.2 Documentation Practices
All validation documentation will adhere to the following practices:
- Entries will be made in indelible ink
- Errors will be crossed out with a single line, initialed, dated, and explained if necessary
- No white-out or erasures are permitted
- Each page will be numbered in "page X of Y" format
- Each document will have appropriate headers and footers
- All pages will be signed and dated by the performer and reviewer
- Electronic records will comply with 21 CFR Part 11 requirements

### 10.3 Records Retention
All validation documents will be retained for the life of the equipment plus one year, or as required by applicable regulations, whichever is longer.
