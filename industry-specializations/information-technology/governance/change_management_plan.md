## 1. Change Management Policy & Framework

### 1.1 Policy
This section outlines the change management policy, ensuring all changes are handled in a structured and efficient manner to mitigate risks and minimize disruption to IT services.

### 1.2 Framework
This framework aligns with ITIL best practices and guides the process of change management from initiation through closure.

---

## 2. Change Advisory Board (CAB) Structure

### 2.1 Composition
The CAB is composed of key stakeholders representing various functional areas, including IT, business operations, and risk management.

### 2.2 Responsibilities
The CAB is responsible for the review and approval of proposed changes, ensuring alignment with business objectives and risk mitigation.

### 2.3 Meeting Schedule
Regular CAB meetings occur [frequency], with an emergency CAB convened as needed for urgent changes.

---

## 3. Change Categories and Classifications

### 3.1 Standard Changes
Pre-authorized changes with a low risk profile, requiring minimal documentation and bypassing full CAB review.

### 3.2 Normal Changes
Require full documentation and CAB approval following a comprehensive assessment.

### 3.3 Major Changes
High-risk changes that necessitate comprehensive planning, testing, and executive approval.

### 3.4 Emergency Changes
Urgent changes implemented to prevent or address significant business impact, following expedited procedures.

---

## 4. Change Management Process

### 4.1 Request
- Submission of a Request for Change (RFC) with detailed justification and preliminary impact assessment.

### 4.2 Assessment
- Detailed technical and business assessment involving risk analysis and resource planning.

### 4.3 Approval
- Approval levels vary by change type, with significant changes requiring CAB or executive approval.

### 4.4 Implementation
- Execution of changes according to the approved plan, including stakeholder notifications and back-out plans.

### 4.5 Review
- Post-implementation review to assess success, document lessons learned, and update records.

---

## 5. Emergency Change Procedures
Streamlined process for handling urgent changes with minimal delay, requiring immediate evaluation and action.

---

## 6. Change Schedule and Calendar
Maintains a forward schedule of changes, avoiding conflicts and ensuring resources are appropriately allocated.

---

## 7. Risk Assessment and Mitigation
Comprehensive risk analysis supported by a risk management matrix to evaluate and mitigate potential impacts.

---

## 8. Communication Plan
Structured communication strategy to keep stakeholders informed at every stage of the change process.

---

## 9. Testing and Validation
Standards for testing and validation to confirm change effectiveness and system stability post-implementation.

---

## 10. Documentation Requirements
Ensures all changes are well-documented, covering key details from initiation to closure.

---

## 11. Rollback Procedures
Defined rollback strategies in case changes cannot be successfully implemented.

---

## 12. Post-Implementation Review
Review process to evaluate change outcomes, address any issues, and integrate lessons learned into future planning.
