## Overview

This Decision Authority Matrix (DAM) defines who has the authority to make specific types of decisions within the project. It establishes clear accountability and prevents decision-making bottlenecks while ensuring appropriate governance oversight.

---

## Decision Authority Levels

### Level 1: Inform Only
- **Authority:** None
- **Role:** Must be informed of the decision
- **Timing:** After decision is made

### Level 2: Consult
- **Authority:** Input provider
- **Role:** Provides expertise and recommendations
- **Timing:** Before decision is made

### Level 3: Recommend
- **Authority:** Makes recommendations
- **Role:** Analyzes options and proposes solution
- **Timing:** During decision analysis

### Level 4: Approve
- **Authority:** Final approval
- **Role:** Makes the final decision
- **Timing:** At decision point

### Level 5: Accountable
- **Authority:** Ultimate accountability
- **Role:** Owns the outcome and consequences
- **Timing:** Ongoing responsibility

## Escalation Matrix

### Level 1: Project Team Level
- **Decision Authority:** Up to $5K, 1 week schedule impact
- **Decisions:** Daily operational decisions, task assignments, minor scope clarifications
- **Escalation Trigger:** Resource conflicts, technical blockers

### Level 2: Project Manager Level  
- **Decision Authority:** Up to $25K, 1 month schedule impact
- **Decisions:** Resource allocation, vendor selection, scope changes
- **Escalation Trigger:** Budget variance, major scope changes, stakeholder conflicts

### Level 3: Sponsor Level
- **Decision Authority:** Up to $100K, 3 month schedule impact  
- **Decisions:** Project continuation, major scope changes, strategic direction
- **Escalation Trigger:** Project viability, strategic alignment, major risks

### Level 4: Executive Level
- **Decision Authority:** Unlimited
- **Decisions:** Project cancellation, strategic pivots, portfolio impacts
- **Escalation Trigger:** Cross-project dependencies, organizational impact

## Decision-Making Process

### 1. Decision Identification
- [ ] Decision clearly defined and documented
- [ ] Urgency and importance assessed
- [ ] Impact analysis completed
- [ ] Decision category identified

### 2. Stakeholder Analysis
- [ ] Required decision makers identified
- [ ] Consultation requirements determined
- [ ] Information requirements defined
- [ ] Timeline established

### 3. Information Gathering
- [ ] Relevant data collected
- [ ] Options analyzed
- [ ] Recommendations prepared
- [ ] Risk assessment completed

### 4. Decision Making
- [ ] Appropriate authority level engaged
- [ ] Consultation completed
- [ ] Decision made and documented
- [ ] Rationale recorded

### 5. Communication
- [ ] Decision communicated to stakeholders
- [ ] Implementation plan defined
- [ ] Monitoring approach established
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled

## Customization Guidelines

### Industry Adaptations
- **Healthcare:** Add regulatory approval authority levels
- **Finance:** Include compliance review requirements  
- **IT:** Add security and architecture review gates
- **Construction:** Include safety and permit authority levels

### Project Size Adaptations
- **Small Projects:** Simplified 3-level matrix
- **Large Projects:** Extended authority levels with committee structures
- **Program/Portfolio:** Cross-project dependency authority
