## 1. Communication Matrix Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
The Communication Matrix defines who needs what information, when they need it, how it will be delivered, and who is responsible for delivering it throughout the project lifecycle.

### 1.2 Scope
This matrix covers all formal and informal communication requirements for:
- Project stakeholders (internal and external)
- Team members and project participants
- Management and executive leadership
- Vendors and external partners
- End users and customers

### 1.3 Communication Objectives
- Ensure timely and accurate information flow
- Prevent communication gaps and misunderstandings
- Maintain stakeholder engagement and satisfaction
- Support decision-making with relevant information
- Enable proactive issue and risk communication

## 2. Communication Matrix Framework

### 2.1 Matrix Elements
| Element | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Stakeholder** | Who needs the information | Project team, sponsor, users |
| **Information Need** | What information they require | Status, decisions, risks, changes |
| **Purpose** | Why they need this information | Decision-making, awareness, action |
| **Frequency** | How often communication occurs | Daily, weekly, monthly, ad-hoc |
| **Method** | How information is delivered | Email, meeting, report, dashboard |
| **Format** | Structure and level of detail | Executive summary, detailed report |
| **Sender** | Who provides the information | PM, team lead, specialist |
| **Timing** | When communication should occur | Specific dates, triggers, milestones |

### 2.2 Communication Types
- **Informational**: Status updates, announcements, notifications
- **Consultative**: Seeking input, feedback, and recommendations
- **Collaborative**: Joint planning, problem-solving, decision-making
- **Directive**: Instructions, approvals, authorizations
- **Escalation**: Issue resolution, conflict management, exception handling

## 4. Communication Methods and Channels

### 4.1 Communication Methods

#### Meetings
- **Face-to-Face**: High-importance decisions, conflict resolution
- **Video Conference**: Regular team meetings, remote collaboration
- **Phone Calls**: Urgent issues, quick clarifications

#### Written Communication
- **Email**: Formal notifications, document sharing, confirmations
- **Reports**: Status updates, dashboards, formal documentation
- **Instant Messaging**: Quick questions, informal coordination

#### Digital Platforms
- **Project Management Tools**: Task updates, progress tracking
- **Collaboration Platforms**: Document sharing, team coordination
- **Dashboards**: Real-time status, metrics visualization

## 9. Crisis Communication Protocol

### 9.1 Crisis Communication Framework

| Crisis Level | Response Time | Approval Required | Communication Method |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Level 1 - Low** | 4 hours | PM | Email notification |
| **Level 2 - Medium** | 2 hours | Sponsor | Email + phone call |
| **Level 3 - High** | 1 hour | Sponsor + executive | All channels |
| **Level 4 - Critical** | 30 minutes | Executive team | Emergency protocol |

### 9.2 Crisis Communication Steps

1. **Assess Situation**: Determine crisis level and impact
2. **Notify Key Stakeholders**: Use appropriate urgency level
3. **Gather Information**: Collect facts and develop response
4. **Communicate Status**: Provide initial communication
5. **Provide Updates**: Regular status updates until resolved
6. **Document Lessons**: Capture learnings for future improvement
