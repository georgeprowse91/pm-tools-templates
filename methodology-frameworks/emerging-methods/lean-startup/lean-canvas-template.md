# Lean Canvas Template

## Overview
The Lean Canvas is a 1-page business model that helps you deconstruct your idea into its key assumptions. It's adapted from Alex Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas for the Lean Startup methodology.

## Canvas Structure

### 1. PROBLEM
**Top 3 problems you're solving**

```
Problem #1: [Most important problem]
- Who has this problem?
- How do they solve it today?
- How painful is this problem (1-10)?

Problem #2: [Second most important]
- Who has this problem?
- How do they solve it today?
- How painful is this problem (1-10)?

Problem #3: [Third most important]
- Who has this problem?
- How do they solve it today?
- How painful is this problem (1-10)?
```

**Existing Alternatives**
- Alternative 1: [How customers solve this today]
- Alternative 2: [Current workarounds]
- Alternative 3: [Competing solutions]

### 2. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS
**Target customers and users**

#### Early Adopters
```
Segment 1: [Most important customer segment]
- Demographics: [Age, income, location, etc.]
- Psychographics: [Attitudes, interests, behaviors]
- Size of segment: [Number of potential customers]
- Accessibility: [How easy to reach them?]

Segment 2: [Second most important]
- Demographics:
- Psychographics:
- Size of segment:
- Accessibility:
```

#### Customer Characteristics
- [ ] Have the problem urgently
- [ ] Are aware they have the problem
- [ ] Have budget/authority to buy
- [ ] Are accessible through your channels

### 3. UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION
**Single, clear compelling message**

```
Primary Value Proposition:
"[For target customer] who [statement of need/opportunity], 
our [product/service] is [product category] that [key benefit]. 
Unlike [primary competitive alternative], we [primary differentiation]."

High-Level Concept:
[Analogy or high-concept pitch - "X for Y" or comparison]
```

#### Key Benefits
1. [Most important benefit]
2. [Second most important benefit]
3. [Third most important benefit]

### 9. UNFAIR ADVANTAGE
**Something that can't be easily copied or bought**

#### Potential Advantages
- [ ] **Network effects**: Value increases with more users
- [ ] **Brand**: Strong brand recognition and trust
- [ ] **Personal authority**: Industry expertise/reputation
- [ ] **Large network**: Existing relationships/partnerships
- [ ] **Expert knowledge**: Specialized domain expertise
- [ ] **Dream team**: Exceptional team with proven track record
- [ ] **Insider information**: Unique market insights
- [ ] **Patents/IP**: Protected intellectual property

```
Primary Unfair Advantage: [Most defensible advantage]
- Description: [Why is this advantage?]
- Sustainability: [How long will this last?]
- Barriers to replication: [What prevents copying?]
```
