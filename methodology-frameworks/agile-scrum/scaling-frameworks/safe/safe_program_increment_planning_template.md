## üìã PI Planning Overview

Program Increment (PI) Planning is a cadence-based, face-to-face event that serves as the heartbeat of the Agile Release Train (ART), aligning all teams to a shared mission and vision. This two-day event brings together all ART members to:

- Align on a common mission and vision
- Identify dependencies and foster cross-team collaboration
- Commit to PI objectives with business value
- Address risks and impediments
- Create team and program plans for the upcoming PI

## üîÑ PI Planning Preparation Checklist

### 2-4 Weeks Before

- [ ] Confirm PI Planning dates and location
- [ ] Reserve venue with adequate breakout spaces
- [ ] Prepare virtual meeting setup if needed
- [ ] Send calendar invitations to all participants
- [ ] Engage with Product Management on vision and priorities
- [ ] Begin preparing feature backlog

### 1-2 Weeks Before

- [ ] Finalize and prioritize Program Backlog
- [ ] Refine Features to INVEST criteria
- [ ] Prepare Business Context presentation
- [ ] Finalize Architecture Vision presentation
- [ ] Create/update Planning Board for dependency tracking
- [ ] Prepare Team Breakout rooms/areas
- [ ] Ensure teams have completed pre-PI Planning backlog refinement
- [ ] Distribute agenda to all participants

### 1-3 Days Before

- [ ] Confirm logistics (room setup, catering, technology)
- [ ] Print physical materials (if applicable)
- [ ] Test all technology platforms
- [ ] Final review of Feature readiness
- [ ] Prepare Risk and Impediment tracking mechanisms
- [ ] Brief facilitators and Scrum Masters on responsibilities

## Appendix A: Role Definitions

- **RTE (Release Train Engineer):** Facilitates the ART process and events, escalates impediments
- **Product Management:** Defines features and participates in planning to provide clarification
- **System Architect/Engineering:** Establishes technical direction and assists teams during planning
- **Business Owners:** Participate in planning, approve plans, and assign business value
- **Scrum Master:** Facilitates team planning, helps coordinate with other teams
- **Product Owner:** Works with teams to define stories and acceptance criteria
- **Development Team:** Defines user stories, estimates, identifies risks and dependencies

## Appendix B: PI Planning Success Factors

1. **Face-to-face participation** (or high-quality virtual experience)
2. **Proper preparation** (features ready, capacity planning done)
3. **Right participants** (all team members, leadership, stakeholders)
4. **Focus on business outcomes** (not just features)
5. **Real-time decision-making** (business owners present)
6. **Commitment-based planning** (not assignment)
7. **Continuous adjustments** during planning
8. **Fast feedback** on plan quality (confidence vote)
