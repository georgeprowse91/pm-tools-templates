# Change Vision Canvas

## Overview
The Change Vision Canvas is an Agile tool for collaboratively creating a shared vision of organizational change. It helps teams quickly align on the purpose, scope, and approach for change initiatives using a visual, collaborative format.

## How to Use This Canvas

### Workshop Format
1. **Preparation** (15 minutes)
   - Gather diverse stakeholders (6-8 people max)
   - Prepare canvas on whiteboard or digital tool
   - Set collaborative ground rules

2. **Canvas Completion** (90 minutes)
   - Work through each section collaboratively
   - Use sticky notes for ideas
   - Time-box each section (10 minutes per box)
   - Encourage building on others' ideas

3. **Synthesis** (15 minutes)
   - Review completed canvas
   - Identify key themes and priorities
   - Agree on next steps

### Section Guidelines

#### 1. Why Change? (Pain Points)
**Purpose**: Establish the burning platform and motivation for change

**Questions to explore**:
- What problems are we trying to solve?
- What opportunities are we missing?
- What's the cost of not changing?
- What's driving the urgency?

**Tips**:
- Focus on observable, specific issues
- Include both problems and opportunities
- Keep it user-centric
- Use data and stories

#### 2. Change Vision (Future State)
**Purpose**: Create a compelling picture of the desired future state

**Questions to explore**:
- What will success look like?
- How will things be different?
- What value will we create?
- What's our elevator pitch?

**Tips**:
- Make it inspiring and achievable
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Use visual language
- Keep it simple and memorable

#### 3. Success Looks Like (Metrics)
**Purpose**: Define measurable indicators of successful change

**Questions to explore**:
- How will we know we've succeeded?
- What metrics matter most?
- What's our definition of done?
- How will we measure adoption?

**Tips**:
- Use leading and lagging indicators
- Include both quantitative and qualitative measures
- Make metrics actionable
- Set realistic targets

#### 4. Who Is Impacted? (Stakeholders)
**Purpose**: Identify all people affected by the change

**Questions to explore**:
- Who will use the new way of working?
- Who influences the success?
- Who might resist the change?
- Who are our change champions?

**Tips**:
- Consider direct and indirect impacts
- Group similar stakeholders
- Note their current pain points
- Identify their motivations

#### 5. Change Approach (Implementation)
**Purpose**: Define how change will be implemented using Agile principles

**Questions to explore**:
- How will we deliver incrementally?
- What's our learning strategy?
- How will we adapt as we go?
- What's our minimum viable change?

**Tips**:
- Emphasize experimentation
- Plan for iteration and learning
- Start small and scale
- Build in feedback loops

#### 6. What Could Go Wrong? (Risks)
**Purpose**: Anticipate potential obstacles and resistance

**Questions to explore**:
- What are our biggest risks?
- Where might we face resistance?
- What dependencies concern us?
- What assumptions might be wrong?

**Tips**:
- Be realistic but not pessimistic
- Focus on controllable risks
- Consider both technical and people risks
- Think about mitigation strategies

#### 7. Change Team (Roles)
**Purpose**: Identify key people and their roles in the change

**Questions to explore**:
- Who will lead the change?
- Who will do the work?
- Who will remove obstacles?
- Who will provide feedback?

**Tips**:
- Keep the team small and empowered
- Include diverse perspectives
- Define clear responsibilities
- Ensure decision-making authority

#### 8. First Steps (Initial Actions)
**Purpose**: Plan immediate next actions to start the change

**Questions to explore**:
- What can we do this week?
- What's our first experiment?
- Who will we start with?
- What do we need to learn first?

**Tips**:
- Focus on learning over building
- Start with willing participants
- Make first steps visible
- Keep initial scope small

#### 9. Feedback Loops (Learning)
**Purpose**: Design mechanisms for continuous learning and adaptation

**Questions to explore**:
- How will we collect feedback?
- When will we reflect and adapt?
- What data will we gather?
- How will we share learnings?

**Tips**:
- Build in regular check-points
- Use multiple feedback channels
- Make data collection easy
- Create safe spaces for honest feedback

## Example: Digital Transformation Change Canvas

### 1. Why Change? (Pain Points)
- Manual processes causing delays
- Customer complaints about responsiveness
- Competitive pressure from digital-first companies
- Employee frustration with outdated tools
- Data silos preventing insights

### 2. Change Vision (Future State)
"Enable seamless digital experiences for customers and employees through automated processes, real-time data access, and intuitive tools that increase satisfaction and productivity."

### 3. Success Looks Like (Metrics)
- 50% reduction in process cycle time
- 90% employee satisfaction with new tools
- 40% increase in customer satisfaction scores
- 100% adoption within 6 months
- Zero critical incidents in first 3 months

### 4. Who Is Impacted? (Stakeholders)
- **Primary**: Customer service team (25 people)
- **Secondary**: IT support, customers, management
- **Influencers**: Department heads, union representatives
- **Champions**: Tech-savvy team members, early adopters

### 5. Change Approach (Implementation)
- 2-week sprints with incremental rollout
- Start with pilot group of 5 volunteers
- User story-driven feature development
- Continuous user testing and feedback
- Weekly demos and retrospectives

### 6. What Could Go Wrong? (Risks)
- Resistance from long-term employees
- Integration issues with legacy systems
- Insufficient training time
- Competing priorities during busy season
- Budget constraints for additional tools

### 7. Change Team (Roles)
- **Product Owner**: Sarah (Business Analyst)
- **Scrum Master**: Mike (Change Manager)
- **Development Team**: IT team + selected users
- **Stakeholders**: Department managers
- **Champions**: 2-3 enthusiastic users per department

### 8. First Steps (Initial Actions)
- Week 1: Form change team and set up workspace
- Week 2: User story mapping workshop
- Week 3: Select and onboard pilot group
- Week 4: First sprint planning session
- Week 5: Begin first 2-week sprint

### 9. Feedback Loops (Learning)
- Daily standups for change team
- Bi-weekly sprint reviews with stakeholders
- Monthly satisfaction surveys
- Quarterly retrospectives with all users
- Real-time usage analytics dashboard

## Digital Tools for Virtual Workshops

### Recommended Platforms
- **Miro**: Interactive whiteboard with canvas template
- **Mural**: Collaborative workspace with sticky notes
- **Figma**: Design tool with real-time collaboration
- **Google Jamboard**: Simple digital whiteboard
- **Microsoft Whiteboard**: Integrated with Office 365

### Virtual Workshop Tips
1. Send canvas template in advance
2. Use breakout rooms for small group discussions
3. Assign a facilitator for each canvas section
4. Use timer to keep discussions focused
5. Take photos/screenshots for documentation
6. Follow up with digital summary

## Integration with Agile Practices

### Sprint Planning
- Use canvas to prioritize change backlog items
- Reference success metrics when defining sprint goals
- Consider stakeholder impact when planning releases

### Daily Standups
- Check progress against first steps
- Identify obstacles from risk section
- Celebrate progress toward vision

### Sprint Reviews
- Demonstrate progress using success metrics
- Gather stakeholder feedback on change approach
- Update canvas based on new learnings

### Retrospectives
- Reflect on effectiveness of feedback loops
- Adjust change approach based on what's working
- Update risk assessment and mitigation strategies
