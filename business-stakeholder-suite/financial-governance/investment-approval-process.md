# Investment Approval Process Template

## Template Overview
A comprehensive framework for evaluating, approving, and monitoring organizational investments across projects, initiatives, and strategic endeavors. This process ensures proper due diligence, risk assessment, and value optimization for all investment decisions.

## Investment Approval Framework

### 1. Investment Classification and Thresholds

#### Investment Categories
```
Strategic Investments:
- Market expansion initiatives
- New product/service development
- Technology platforms
- Acquisition and partnerships
- Transformation programs

Operational Investments:
- Process improvement projects
- System upgrades/replacements
- Infrastructure enhancements
- Compliance initiatives
- Risk mitigation projects

Maintenance Investments:
- Asset replacement/renewal
- System maintenance
- Facility improvements
- Regulatory compliance
- Safety enhancements
```

#### Approval Thresholds
```
Investment Level | Amount Range    | Approval Authority | Required Documentation
Level 1         | < $25K         | Department Manager | Basic business case
Level 2         | $25K - $100K   | Director          | Standard business case
Level 3         | $100K - $500K  | VP + Finance      | Comprehensive case
Level 4         | $500K - $2M    | Executive Team    | Full investment proposal
Level 5         | > $2M          | Board of Directors| Strategic investment plan

Note: Thresholds should be adjusted based on organizational size and industry
```

### 2. Investment Request Process

#### Request Initiation
```
Investment Request Form:
- Project/Initiative Title: [Name]
- Requesting Department: [Department]
- Business Sponsor: [Name/Role]
- Requested Amount: $[Amount]
- Investment Category: [Strategic/Operational/Maintenance]

Initial Screening:
- Strategic alignment verification

```

#### Documentation Requirements
```
Level 1 - Basic Business Case:
- Problem/opportunity statement
- Proposed solution overview
- Cost estimate and timeline
- Expected benefits summary
- Risk assessment (high-level)

Level 2 - Standard Business Case:
- Detailed problem analysis
- Solution alternatives evaluation
- Financial analysis (ROI, NPV)
- Implementation plan
- Risk register and mitigation

Level 3 - Comprehensive Case:
- Market/competitive analysis
- Technical feasibility study
- Detailed financial model
- Resource requirements
- Change management plan

Level 4 - Full Investment Proposal:
- Strategic context and alignment
- Business case validation
- Technical architecture
- Implementation roadmap
- Performance measurement plan

Level 5 - Strategic Investment Plan:
- Strategic impact assessment
- Portfolio integration analysis
- Stakeholder impact evaluation
- Long-term sustainability plan
- Board presentation materials
```

### 3. Evaluation Criteria and Scoring

#### Financial Evaluation
```
Financial Metrics:
- Net Present Value (NPV): Discounted cash flows
- Internal Rate of Return (IRR): Return percentage
- Return on Investment (ROI): Benefit/cost ratio
- Payback Period: Time to recover investment
- Economic Value Added (EVA): Value creation measure

Scoring Framework:
NPV Score:
- NPV > $1M: 10 points
- NPV $500K-$1M: 8 points
- NPV $100K-$500K: 6 points
- NPV $0-$100K: 4 points
- NPV < $0: 0 points

IRR Score:
- IRR > 25%: 10 points
- IRR 20-25%: 8 points
- IRR 15-20%: 6 points
- IRR 10-15%: 4 points
- IRR < 10%: 2 points
```

#### Strategic Evaluation
```
Strategic Alignment Criteria:
- Mission/vision alignment: [1-5 scale]
- Strategic objective support: [1-5 scale]
- Competitive advantage creation: [1-5 scale]
- Market positioning impact: [1-5 scale]
- Innovation contribution: [1-5 scale]

Strategic Scoring:
Score 5: Critical for strategic success
Score 4: High strategic value
Score 3: Moderate strategic benefit
Score 2: Limited strategic impact
Score 1: Minimal strategic value
```

#### Risk Assessment
```
Risk Categories:
- Technical Risk: Complexity, feasibility, integration
- Market Risk: Competition, demand, timing
- Financial Risk: Cost overruns, benefit realization
- Operational Risk: Disruption, capability gaps
- Regulatory Risk: Compliance, approval delays

Risk Scoring:
Low Risk (1-3): Well-understood, proven solutions
Medium Risk (4-6): Some uncertainties, manageable
High Risk (7-10): Significant uncertainties, complex

Risk-Adjusted Score = Base Score Ã— (1 - Risk Factor/10)
```

### 4. Approval Workflow

#### Standard Approval Process
```
Step 1: Initial Review (2-3 days)
- Completeness check
- Threshold verification
- Initial feasibility assessment
- Resource availability review

Step 2: Technical Evaluation (5-7 days)
- Technical feasibility review
- Architecture compliance
- Integration assessment
- Security evaluation

Step 3: Financial Analysis (3-5 days)
- Financial model validation
- Cost-benefit verification
- Budget impact assessment
- Funding source identification

Step 4: Risk Assessment (3-5 days)
- Risk identification and analysis
- Mitigation strategy evaluation
- Contingency planning review
- Compliance verification

Step 5: Committee Review (7-10 days)
- Investment committee evaluation
- Cross-functional input
- Portfolio impact assessment
- Recommendation development

Step 6: Final Approval (2-5 days)
- Approving authority decision
- Conditions and constraints
- Resource allocation
- Monitoring requirements
```

#### Fast-Track Process
```
Criteria for Fast-Track:
- Urgent business need
- Regulatory requirement
- Risk mitigation necessity
- Time-sensitive opportunity
- Small investment amount

Fast-Track Timeline:
- Documentation: Simplified
- Review: Accelerated (2-3 days)
- Approval: Single authority
- Conditions: Standard monitoring
```

### 5. Investment Committee Structure

#### Committee Composition
```
Investment Committee Members:
- Chief Executive Officer (Chair)
- Chief Financial Officer
- Chief Technology Officer
- Chief Operating Officer
- Business Unit Leaders
- Project Management Office Director

Advisory Members:
- Legal Counsel
- Risk Management Director
- Human Resources Director
- Subject Matter Experts (as needed)
- External Advisors (for large investments)
```

#### Committee Responsibilities
```
Decision Making:
- Evaluate investment proposals
- Approve/reject/defer decisions
- Set conditions and constraints
- Allocate resources and funding
- Monitor investment performance

Governance:
- Establish investment policies
- Define approval criteria
- Review process effectiveness
- Ensure strategic alignment
- Manage portfolio balance

Oversight:
- Monitor approved investments
- Review performance reports
- Address escalated issues
- Approve scope changes
- Conduct post-investment reviews
```

### 7. Risk Management

#### Risk Identification
```
Investment Risk Categories:
- Strategic Risks: Market changes, competitive response
- Execution Risks: Project delivery, capability gaps
- Financial Risks: Cost overruns, revenue shortfalls
- Technology Risks: Technical failures, integration issues
- Regulatory Risks: Compliance failures, policy changes
- Operational Risks: Business disruption, resource constraints
```

#### Risk Mitigation Strategies
```
Prevention Strategies:
- Thorough due diligence
- Pilot programs and proof-of-concepts
- Phased implementation approach
- Expert advisory support
- Compliance verification

Contingency Planning:
- Alternative solution options
- Escalation procedures
- Resource reallocation plans
- Exit strategies
- Recovery procedures
```

### 8. Investment Monitoring and Control

#### Performance Tracking
```
Key Performance Indicators:
- Financial Performance: Actual vs. projected ROI, NPV
- Schedule Performance: Milestones, timeline adherence
- Scope Performance: Deliverable completion
- Quality Performance: Success criteria achievement
- Risk Performance: Risk mitigation effectiveness

Monitoring Framework:
- Monthly: Project progress, budget status
- Quarterly: Performance against business case
- Semi-annually: Strategic alignment review
- Annually: Comprehensive investment assessment
```

#### Reporting Requirements
```
Progress Reports:
- Executive dashboard updates
- Investment committee reports
- Stakeholder communications
- Board reporting (for large investments)
- Portfolio performance summaries

Exception Reporting:
- Budget variance alerts
- Schedule delay notifications
- Scope change requests
- Risk escalations
- Quality issues
```

### 9. Portfolio Management

#### Portfolio Optimization
```
Portfolio Balance:
- Risk distribution across investments
- Strategic initiative alignment
- Resource allocation optimization
- Timeline coordination
- Benefit realization sequencing

Portfolio Reviews:
- Quarterly portfolio assessments
- Annual strategic alignment review
- Investment performance ranking
- Resource reallocation decisions
- Portfolio optimization recommendations
```

#### Investment Prioritization
```
Prioritization Criteria:
1. Strategic Importance (Weight: 40%)
2. Financial Return (Weight: 30%)
3. Risk Level (Weight: 15%)
4. Resource Requirements (Weight: 10%)
5. Implementation Complexity (Weight: 5%)

Scoring Matrix:
Investment | Strategic | Financial | Risk | Resources | Complexity | Total Score
INV-001   | 8        | 7        | 6    | 8        | 7         | 7.4
INV-002   | 9        | 6        | 8    | 7        | 6         | 7.6
```

### 10. Change Management

#### Investment Changes
```
Change Categories:
- Scope changes: Deliverable modifications
- Budget changes: Cost increase/decrease
- Timeline changes: Schedule adjustments
- Resource changes: Team modifications
- Objective changes: Benefit adjustments

Change Control Process:
1. Change request submission
2. Impact analysis
3. Stakeholder consultation
4. Approval authority decision
5. Implementation and communication
```

#### Re-approval Triggers
```
Major Change Thresholds:
- Budget increase > 15%
- Timeline extension > 20%
- Scope reduction > 25%
- Risk profile change (significant)
- Strategic objective modification

Re-approval Process:
- Updated business case
- Fresh evaluation process
- Committee re-review
- New approval decision
- Revised monitoring plan
```

### 11. Post-Investment Review

#### Review Process
```
Review Timeline:
- 6 months post-implementation: Initial benefits assessment
- 12 months post-implementation: Comprehensive review
- 24 months post-implementation: Long-term impact evaluation
- Annual reviews: Ongoing value assessment

Review Components:
- Actual vs. projected performance
- Lessons learned documentation
- Best practices identification
- Process improvement recommendations
- Future investment insights
```

#### Success Metrics
```
Investment Success Indicators:
- Financial targets achieved
- Strategic objectives delivered
- Timeline and budget adherence
- Quality standards met
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Risk management effectiveness

Organizational Learning:
- Process improvements identified
- Capability enhancements achieved
- Knowledge transfer completed
- Best practices documented
- Future investment criteria refined
```
