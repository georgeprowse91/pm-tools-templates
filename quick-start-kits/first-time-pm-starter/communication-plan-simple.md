## Purpose of This Document

This communication plan helps you identify who needs what information during your project, how they'll receive it, and when. Clear communication prevents misunderstandings and keeps everyone aligned.

## Project Communication Goals

- Keep all stakeholders informed about project progress
- Ensure team members understand their responsibilities
- Identify and address issues quickly
- Maintain transparency about project status
- Build trust with stakeholders through consistent updates

## Communication Matrix

| Stakeholder Group | Information Needs | Communication Method | Frequency | Owner | Notes |
|------------------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Project Sponsor | Project status, budget updates, major issues | Email report, 1:1 meeting | Weekly | Project Manager | Keep concise, focus on decisions needed |
| Team Members | Task assignments, schedule changes, technical decisions | Team meetings, project tool (e.g., Trello, Jira) | Daily standup, as needed | Project Manager | Include next steps and action items |
| Department Heads | Resource usage, cross-department impacts | Email summary | Bi-weekly | Project Manager | Highlight resource needs or conflicts |
| End Users | Feature updates, training information | Email newsletter | Monthly | Project Manager | Use simple language, avoid technical terms |
| External Vendors | Requirements, timeline changes | Email, scheduled calls | As needed | Project Manager | Document all agreements in writing |

## Status Reporting

### Weekly Status Report
- **Distribution**: Project sponsor, department heads
- **Content**: Progress highlights, upcoming milestones, issues/risks, decisions needed
- **Format**: Email with attached 1-page PDF summary
- **Owner**: Project Manager
- **Day Sent**: Every Friday by 3:00 PM

### Daily Team Updates
- **Distribution**: Project team
- **Content**: Tasks completed, tasks in progress, blockers
- **Format**: 15-minute standup meeting or team chat message
- **Owner**: Each team member
- **Time**: Daily at [Time]

## Crisis Communication Procedure

1. **Identify the Issue**: Determine severity and impact
2. **Notify Key Stakeholders**: Contact project sponsor immediately for critical issues
3. **Develop Action Plan**: Create plan to address the issue
4. **Communicate to Wider Team**: Share what happened and next steps
5. **Follow Up**: Provide regular updates until resolution

## Tips for Effective Communication

1. **Be Clear and Concise**: Keep messages short and focused
2. **Know Your Audience**: Adjust technical detail based on who you're talking to
3. **Listen Actively**: Ask questions and confirm understanding
4. **Use Visual Aids**: Charts and graphs can explain complex information quickly
5. **Follow Up**: Document decisions and action items after meetings
6. **Be Consistent**: Send updates on schedule so people know when to expect them
7. **Choose the Right Channel**: Don't use email for urgent matters

---

*Remember: Communication is the most important tool in your project management toolkit. When in doubt, over-communicate rather than under-communicate.*
