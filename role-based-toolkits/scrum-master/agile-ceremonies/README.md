## ðŸŽ¯ Sprint Planning

### Purpose & Goals
**Transform product backlog items into a concrete plan for the upcoming sprint**

**Key Outcomes:**
- Sprint goal established and understood by all
- Sprint backlog created with committed work
- Team confident in their ability to deliver
- Clear understanding of Definition of Done

### **[â†’ Download: Sprint Planning Template](sprint-planning-template.md)**

### Agenda (4 hours for 2-week sprint)
**Part 1: What (2 hours)**
1. **Sprint Goal Discussion** (30 min)
   - Product Owner presents sprint goal
   - Team discusses and clarifies
   - Confirm alignment with product vision

2. **Backlog Review** (90 min)
   - Review prioritized product backlog items
   - Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
   - Estimate effort and complexity

**Part 2: How (2 hours)**
3. **Capacity Planning** (30 min)
   - Review team capacity for sprint
   - Account for holidays, training, meetings
   - Consider historical velocity

4. **Task Breakdown** (90 min)
   - Break backlog items into tasks
   - Identify dependencies and risks
   - Commit to sprint backlog

### Facilitation Tips
- **Keep timeboxed** - Use visible timers
- **Stay focused** - Park off-topic discussions
- **Encourage participation** - Draw out quiet team members
- **Manage scope creep** - Protect sprint goal integrity

## ðŸ‘¥ Daily Standups

### Purpose & Goals
**Synchronize team activities and identify impediments**

**Key Outcomes:**
- Shared understanding of progress
- Impediments surfaced and addressed
- Team collaboration opportunities identified
- Sprint goal progress visible

### **[â†’ Download: Daily Standup Template](daily-standup-template.md)**

### Format (15 minutes maximum)
**For each team member:**
1. **What did I complete yesterday?**
2. **What will I work on today?** 
3. **What impediments are blocking me?**

### Alternative Formats
**Walking the Board:**
- Focus on work items rather than individuals
- Review each story/task from right to left
- Discuss what's needed to move items forward

**Yesterday, Today, Blockers:**
- Traditional three-question format
- Good for new or distributed teams
- Clear structure everyone understands

**Commitment-Based:**
- Focus on sprint goal progress
- What did we complete toward our goal?
- What threatens our sprint commitment?

### Facilitation Techniques
- **Start on time** - Don't wait for latecomers
- **Stand up** - Literally, keeps energy high
- **Use visual aids** - Point to board/burndown
- **Address side conversations** - Take detailed discussions offline
- **Track impediments** - Maintain visible impediment board

## ðŸŽª Sprint Review

### Purpose & Goals
**Demonstrate completed work and gather stakeholder feedback**

**Key Outcomes:**
- Stakeholders see working software/deliverables
- Feedback collected for future planning
- Product backlog updated based on input
- Team celebrates accomplishments

### **[â†’ Download: Sprint Review Template](sprint-review-template.md)**

### Agenda (2 hours for 2-week sprint)
1. **Welcome & Context** (10 min)
   - Review sprint goal and what was planned
   - Set expectations for the demo

2. **Sprint Accomplishments** (15 min)
   - Review completed backlog items
   - Share metrics (velocity, burndown)
   - Highlight team achievements

3. **Product Demonstration** (60 min)
   - Demo working features/deliverables
   - Show real usage scenarios
   - Invite hands-on exploration

4. **Stakeholder Feedback** (30 min)
   - Gather reactions and suggestions
   - Discuss market changes or new requirements
   - Identify opportunities for improvement

5. **Next Steps** (5 min)
   - Preview upcoming sprint priorities
   - Thank participants for feedback

### Demo Best Practices
- **Show real functionality** - Not slides or mockups
- **Tell a story** - Connect features to user value
- **Prepare backup plans** - Have screenshots if live demo fails
- **Encourage interaction** - Let stakeholders try features
- **Focus on value** - Emphasize business impact

## ðŸ”„ Sprint Retrospective

### Purpose & Goals
**Reflect on process and identify improvements**

**Key Outcomes:**
- Team identifies what's working well
- Process improvements identified and planned
- Team commitment to changes
- Psychological safety maintained and strengthened

### **[â†’ Download: Sprint Retrospective Template](sprint-retrospective-template.md)**

### Retrospective Techniques

**Start, Stop, Continue**
- What should we start doing?
- What should we stop doing?
- What should we continue doing?

**Glad, Sad, Mad**
- What made us glad this sprint?
- What made us sad or disappointed?
- What made us mad or frustrated?

**4 L's (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)**
- What did we like about this sprint?
- What did we learn?
- What did we lack or miss?
- What did we long for or wish we had?

**Timeline Retrospective**
- Create timeline of sprint events
- Mark significant moments (positive/negative)
- Discuss patterns and root causes

### Action Planning
1. **Prioritize insights** - Vote on most important items
2. **Create specific actions** - Make improvements concrete
3. **Assign owners** - Who will drive each change?
4. **Set success criteria** - How will we know it worked?
5. **Schedule follow-up** - When will we check progress?

### Facilitation Guidelines
- **Create safety** - Establish ground rules for openness
- **Focus on systems** - Not individuals or blame
- **Generate actions** - Don't just complain, improve
- **Follow through** - Track and review previous actions

## ðŸ“ Backlog Refinement

### Purpose & Goals
**Prepare backlog items for upcoming sprint planning**

**Key Outcomes:**
- Backlog items have clear acceptance criteria
- Effort estimates are updated and accurate
- Dependencies and risks identified
- Items are ready for sprint planning

### **[â†’ Download: Backlog Refinement Template](backlog-refinement-template.md)**

### Ongoing Activities (1-2 hours per week)
1. **Review New Items** (30 min)
   - Understand requirements and scope
   - Break down large items (epics)
   - Add initial effort estimates

2. **Refine Existing Items** (30 min)
   - Add detail to upcoming items
   - Update estimates based on new information
   - Identify acceptance criteria

3. **Remove Stale Items** (15 min)
   - Archive outdated or irrelevant items
   - Clean up duplicates
   - Maintain backlog hygiene

4. **Dependency Mapping** (15 min)
   - Identify cross-team dependencies
   - Plan integration and coordination
   - Surface technical risks

### Definition of Ready Checklist
- [ ] User story format: "As a... I want... So that..."
- [ ] Acceptance criteria clearly defined
- [ ] Effort estimate assigned
- [ ] Dependencies identified
- [ ] Testable and demonstrable
- [ ] Small enough to complete in one sprint

## ðŸ’¡ Pro Tips

### For New Scrum Masters
1. **Master the basics first** - Focus on core ceremonies before advanced techniques
2. **Practice facilitation skills** - Take training or observe experienced facilitators  
3. **Build psychological safety** - This is the foundation of all effective ceremonies

### For Experienced Scrum Masters
1. **Experiment with formats** - Try new retrospective techniques and facilitation methods
2. **Focus on outcomes** - Measure ceremony effectiveness, not just completion
3. **Scale techniques** - Adapt approaches for larger or distributed teams

### For Organizations
1. **Train facilitators** - Invest in Scrum Master development and certification
2. **Standardize basics** - Have consistent ceremony formats across teams
3. **Share best practices** - Create communities of practice for Scrum Masters
