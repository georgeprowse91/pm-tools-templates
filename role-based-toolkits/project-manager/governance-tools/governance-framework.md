# Project Governance Framework Template

## Framework Overview
A comprehensive governance framework that establishes clear structures, processes, and accountability mechanisms for effective project oversight and decision-making. This framework ensures strategic alignment, risk management, and value delivery across all project activities.

## Governance Structure

### 1. Governance Hierarchy

#### Executive Level Governance
```
Project Steering Committee:
- Executive Sponsor (Chair)
- Business Unit Owners
- Chief Financial Officer
- Chief Technology Officer (if applicable)
- Key Stakeholder Representatives

Responsibilities:
- Strategic direction and alignment
- Major decision approval
- Resource allocation and funding
- Risk oversight and mitigation
- Performance monitoring and accountability
- Issue escalation and resolution

Meeting Frequency: Monthly or as needed
Decision Authority: Strategic changes, budget > $X, scope changes > Y%
```

#### Operational Level Governance
```
Project Management Board:
- Project Manager (Chair)
- Work Stream Leaders
- Technical Leads
- Business Analysts
- Quality Assurance Lead

Responsibilities:
- Tactical decision-making
- Resource coordination
- Day-to-day issue resolution
- Progress monitoring and reporting
- Quality assurance oversight
- Risk identification and assessment

Meeting Frequency: Weekly
Decision Authority: Operational changes, resource allocation, minor scope adjustments
```

### 2. Governance Roles and Responsibilities

#### Executive Sponsor
```
Primary Accountabilities:
- Overall project success and value delivery
- Strategic alignment and business case ownership
- Resource commitment and funding authorization
- Stakeholder engagement and communication
- Risk acceptance and major decision approval

Key Activities:
- Project charter approval and sign-off
- Steering committee leadership
- Barrier removal and issue escalation
- Performance review and corrective action
- Change approval for significant impacts
```

#### Project Manager
```
Primary Accountabilities:
- Day-to-day project execution and delivery
- Team leadership and coordination
- Schedule, budget, and scope management
- Risk and issue management
- Stakeholder communication and reporting

Key Activities:
- Project planning and execution oversight
- Team performance management
- Progress monitoring and reporting
- Change management coordination
- Quality assurance and delivery
```

#### Business Owner/Product Owner
```
Primary Accountabilities:
- Business requirements definition and validation
- User acceptance and solution adoption
- Benefit realization and value delivery
- Business process change management
- Stakeholder representation and engagement

Key Activities:
- Requirements gathering and prioritization
- User story definition and acceptance criteria
- Solution testing and validation
- Training and change management support
- Post-implementation benefit measurement
```

### 3. Decision-Making Framework

#### Decision Authority Matrix (RACI)
```
Decision Type | Executive Sponsor | Project Manager | Business Owner | Technical Lead
Project Charter | A | R | C | I
Budget Changes >$X | A | R | C | I
Scope Changes >Y% | A | R | R | C
Technical Architecture | C | C | I | A
Resource Allocation | C | A | C | R
Quality Standards | I | A | R | R
Risk Response | C | A | C | C

Legend:
R = Responsible (does the work)
A = Accountable (ensures completion)
C = Consulted (provides input)
I = Informed (kept updated)
```

#### Decision Escalation Process
```
Level 1: Project Team
- Technical decisions
- Day-to-day operational issues
- Minor schedule adjustments
- Resource coordination

Level 2: Project Management Board
- Scope changes <Y%
- Budget variances <$X
- Resource conflicts
- Quality issues

Level 3: Steering Committee
- Strategic decisions
- Major scope/budget changes
- Cross-functional conflicts
- High-risk issues

Level 4: Executive Leadership
- Project cancellation/continuation
- Major investment decisions
- Organizational impact issues
- Portfolio-level conflicts
```

### 4. Governance Processes

#### Project Initiation Governance
```
Charter Development and Approval:
1. Business case development and validation
2. Initial scope and requirements definition
3. Resource and budget estimation
4. Risk assessment and mitigation planning
5. Stakeholder identification and engagement
6. Success criteria and measurement definition
7. Governance structure establishment
8. Formal charter approval and sign-off

Approval Criteria:
- Strategic alignment verification
- Business case validation
- Resource availability confirmation
- Risk acceptability assessment
- Stakeholder commitment verification
```

#### Ongoing Project Governance
```
Regular Governance Activities:
- Weekly project team coordination
- Bi-weekly progress reviews
- Monthly steering committee meetings
- Quarterly strategic alignment reviews
- As-needed escalation and decision meetings

Governance Checkpoints:
- Phase gate reviews
- Milestone assessments
- Budget and schedule reviews
- Risk and issue evaluations
- Quality and compliance audits
- Stakeholder satisfaction surveys
```

### 5. Performance Management

#### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
```
Project Performance:
- Schedule Performance Index (SPI): EV/PV
- Cost Performance Index (CPI): EV/AC
- Quality Index: Defects/Deliverables
- Scope Completion: Actual/Planned %
- Stakeholder Satisfaction: Survey Score

Governance Effectiveness:
- Decision Cycle Time: Average days to decision
- Issue Resolution Time: Average days to closure
- Change Request Processing: Average approval time
- Meeting Effectiveness: Attendance and outcomes
- Escalation Frequency: Number per month
```

#### Performance Reporting
```
Executive Dashboard:
- Overall project health status
- Key milestone achievement
- Budget and schedule performance
- Top risks and issues
- Critical decisions required

Detailed Reports:
- Comprehensive progress analysis
- Financial performance details
- Resource utilization metrics
- Quality and deliverable status
- Stakeholder feedback summary
```

### 6. Risk and Issue Management

#### Risk Governance Process
```
Risk Identification and Assessment:
- Regular risk identification sessions
- Risk impact and probability assessment
- Risk appetite and tolerance definition
- Risk response strategy development
- Risk ownership assignment

Risk Escalation Thresholds:
- High Impact + High Probability → Steering Committee
- Very High Impact (any probability) → Executive Sponsor
- Portfolio/Strategic Risks → Executive Leadership
- Regulatory/Compliance Risks → Legal/Compliance Team
```

#### Issue Management Process
```
Issue Resolution Workflow:
1. Issue identification and logging
2. Impact assessment and categorization
3. Owner assignment and action planning
4. Resolution execution and monitoring
5. Closure verification and lessons learned

Escalation Triggers:
- Unresolved issues >X days
- Issues impacting critical path
- Cross-functional conflicts
- Resource constraint issues
- Quality or compliance concerns
```

### 7. Change Management

#### Change Control Process
```
Change Request Management:
1. Change identification and documentation
2. Impact assessment (scope, schedule, budget, risk)
3. Stakeholder consultation and input
4. Decision authority evaluation
5. Approval/rejection decision
6. Implementation planning and execution
7. Communication and documentation

Change Control Board:
- Project Manager (Chair)
- Business Owner
- Technical Lead
- Quality Lead
- Finance Representative (for budget impacts)
```

#### Change Categories and Approval Authority
```
Change Type | Approval Authority | Process
Minor (<$X, <Y% schedule) | Project Manager | Streamlined
Moderate ($X-$Z, Y%-Z%) | Change Control Board | Standard
Major (>$Z, >Z%) | Steering Committee | Comprehensive
Strategic/Scope | Executive Sponsor | Full Assessment
```

```
Maturity Levels:

Level 2: Managed - Basic governance structure in place
Level 3: Defined - Standardized governance processes
Level 4: Quantitatively Managed - Metrics-driven governance
Level 5: Optimizing - Continuous improvement focus

Assessment Areas:
- Governance structure effectiveness
- Decision-making efficiency
- Risk management maturity
- Change management capability
- Communication effectiveness
```
