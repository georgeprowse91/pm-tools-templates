## 1. Stakeholder Mapping Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This template helps identify, categorize, and map all project stakeholders to understand their influence, interest, and engagement requirements.

### 1.2 Stakeholder Definition
A stakeholder is any individual, group, or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by the project.

### 1.3 Benefits of Stakeholder Mapping
- Identifies all relevant stakeholders
- Understands stakeholder needs and expectations
- Plans appropriate engagement strategies
- Prevents stakeholder-related risks
- Improves project success rates

### 5.2 Engagement Levels
- **Unaware**: No knowledge of project
- **Resistant**: Aware but opposed to project
- **Neutral**: Aware but neither supportive nor opposed
- **Supportive**: Aware and supportive of project
- **Leading**: Aware and actively supporting project

### 5.3 Engagement Strategies
- **Information**: Provide regular updates and information
- **Consultation**: Seek input on decisions affecting them
- **Involvement**: Include in planning and decision-making
- **Collaboration**: Partner in implementation
- **Empowerment**: Give authority and accountability

## 10. Best Practices

### 10.1 Stakeholder Identification
- Cast a wide net initially
- Use multiple identification techniques
- Review and update regularly
- Consider indirect stakeholders
- Validate with team members

### 10.2 Stakeholder Engagement
- Understand their perspective
- Communicate value proposition
- Be transparent and honest
- Listen actively
- Follow through on commitments
- Adapt communication style
- Build relationships early

### 10.3 Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Ignoring low-power stakeholders
- Over-communicating to some, under-communicating to others
- Assuming stakeholder positions are static
- Focusing only on positive stakeholders
- Not addressing concerns proactively

## 11. Review and Updates

### 11.1 Review Triggers
- Project phase changes
- Scope changes
- Organizational changes
- Stakeholder feedback
- Risk materialization

### 11.2 Update Process
1. Review current stakeholder map
2. Identify new stakeholders
3. Reassess power and interest levels
4. Update engagement strategies
5. Communicate changes to team
6. Document lessons learned
