## 1. Stakeholder Analysis Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This document provides a comprehensive analysis of project stakeholders, their interests, influence, and engagement strategies to ensure project success.

### 1.2 Scope
This analysis covers:
- All identified project stakeholders
- Stakeholder interests and concerns
- Power and influence assessment
- Engagement strategies and tactics
- Communication requirements
- Risk assessment and mitigation

### 1.3 Analysis Framework
The stakeholder analysis uses multiple frameworks:
- **Power/Interest Grid**: Categorizes stakeholders by influence and interest
- **Stakeholder Attitude Scale**: Measures support level
- **Influence Network Map**: Shows stakeholder relationships
- **Engagement Strategy Matrix**: Plans specific engagement approaches

- **Champion (5)**: Actively promotes and supports the project
- **Supporter (4)**: Generally supportive with minor concerns
- **Neutral (3)**: No strong opinion either way
- **Skeptic (2)**: Has concerns but not actively opposing

### 4.3 Attitude Change Strategies
**For Opponents and Skeptics:**
- **Understand Concerns**: Deep dive into their specific objections
- **Address Issues**: Develop specific responses to their concerns
- **Find Common Ground**: Identify shared interests or goals
- **Gradual Engagement**: Build trust through small wins

**For Neutral Stakeholders:**
- **Education**: Provide information about project benefits
- **Involvement**: Include them in planning and decision-making
- **Quick Wins**: Demonstrate early value to build confidence
- **Peer Influence**: Use supportive stakeholders to influence

### 9.2 Early Warning Indicators
- **Engagement Decline**: Reduced participation in meetings or communications
- **Negative Feedback**: Critical comments or resistance to project activities
- **Resource Issues**: Delayed approvals or resource allocation problems
- **Political Changes**: Organizational changes affecting stakeholder positions

### 9.3 Contingency Plans
**If Key Supporter Becomes Opponent:**
- Immediate meeting to understand concerns
- Develop specific response to address issues
- Engage their influencers to help mediate
- Escalate to higher authority if necessary

**If Critical Stakeholder Leaves Organization:**
- Quick knowledge transfer session
- Identify and engage replacement stakeholder
- Brief new stakeholder on project context
- Rebuild relationship and support
