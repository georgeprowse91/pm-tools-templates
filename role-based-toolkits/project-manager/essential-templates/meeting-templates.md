# Meeting Templates Collection

## Overview

Effective meetings are crucial for project success. This collection provides structured templates for common project meetings to ensure they are focused, productive, and achieve their intended outcomes.

**Purpose**: Provide standardized formats and agendas for essential project meetings

**When to Use**: Throughout all project phases for regular and ad-hoc meetings

**Who Uses**: Project Managers, Team Members, Stakeholders

   - What are their roles and responsibilities?
   - How will decisions be made?

4. **Project Approach**
   - What methodology will we use?
   - What are our key processes?
   - How will we handle changes?

5. **Communication & Collaboration**
   - How will we communicate?

### Attendees
**Required**:
- [ ] Development Team
- [ ] Scrum Master
- [ ] Product Owner (optional)

### Meeting Agenda

| Time | Duration | Topic | Method | Outcome |
|------|----------|-------|--------|---------|
| [Time] | 5 min | Check-in | Round robin | Team engagement |
| [Time] | 15 min | What Went Well | Appreciation circle | Positive reinforcement |
| [Time] | 15 min | What Didn't Go Well | Problem identification | Issue awareness |
| [Time] | 15 min | What We Learned | Insight sharing | Knowledge capture |
| [Time] | 15 min | What to Try Next | Action planning | Improvement commitments |
| [Time] | 5 min | Closing & Actions | Summary | Clear next steps |

**Total Duration**: 70 minutes

### Retrospective Techniques

**Technique 1: Start-Stop-Continue**
- **Start**: What should we start doing?
- **Stop**: What should we stop doing?
- **Continue**: What should we continue doing?

**Technique 2: Mad-Sad-Glad**
- **Mad**: What frustrated us?
- **Sad**: What disappointed us?
- **Glad**: What made us happy?

**Technique 3: 4 L's**
- **Liked**: What did we like?
- **Learned**: What did we learn?
- **Lacked**: What did we lack?
- **Longed For**: What did we long for?

## 10. Daily Standup Meeting Template

### Meeting Information
- **Meeting Title**: Daily Standup
- **Time**: [Same time daily]
- **Duration**: 15 minutes maximum
- **Location**: [Consistent location]
- **Facilitator**: [Scrum Master/Team Lead]

### Attendees
**Required**:
- [ ] Development Team
- [ ] Scrum Master/Team Lead

**Optional**:
- [ ] Product Owner
- [ ] Stakeholders (observers only)

### Meeting Format

**For Each Team Member** (2-3 minutes each):
1. **What did I complete yesterday?**
   - Focus on work items completed
   - Connect to sprint/project goals

2. **What will I work on today?**
   - Specific tasks or objectives
   - Commitment for the day

3. **Do I have any blockers or impediments?**
   - Obstacles preventing progress
   - Help needed from team

### Facilitator Guidelines
- **Keep it time-boxed**: Maximum 15 minutes
- **Stay focused**: Three questions only
- **Address blockers**: Note impediments for follow-up
- **Encourage collaboration**: Team coordination
- **Avoid problem-solving**: Take detailed discussions offline

### Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- ❌ Status reporting to the manager
- ❌ Detailed problem-solving discussions
- ❌ Long explanations of technical details
- ❌ Blame or criticism
- ❌ Missing team members regularly

### Follow-Up Actions
- [ ] Schedule detailed discussions for blockers
- [ ] Update project tracking tools
- [ ] Communicate impediments to stakeholders
- [ ] Adjust daily plans based on team input

## Meeting Best Practices

### General Guidelines

#### Before the Meeting
- [ ] Send agenda 24-48 hours in advance
- [ ] Include all necessary background materials
- [ ] Confirm attendee availability
- [ ] Test technology and equipment
- [ ] Prepare materials and handouts

#### During the Meeting
- [ ] Start and end on time
- [ ] Follow the agenda
- [ ] Encourage participation from all attendees
- [ ] Take clear notes and action items
- [ ] Summarize key decisions and next steps

#### After the Meeting
- [ ] Send meeting notes within 24 hours
- [ ] Distribute action items with owners and due dates
- [ ] Update project documentation
- [ ] Schedule follow-up meetings as needed
- [ ] File meeting records appropriately

### Virtual Meeting Tips
- [ ] Test technology before the meeting
- [ ] Use video when possible
- [ ] Mute when not speaking
- [ ] Share screens effectively
- [ ] Use collaboration tools (polls, whiteboard)
- [ ] Take frequent engagement checks
