## Team Norms and Guidelines

### Working Agreements
#### Collaboration Principles
- **Respect**: All team members treat each other with respect and professionalism
- **Transparency**: Open and honest communication about progress, challenges, and concerns
- **Accountability**: Each team member takes ownership of their commitments
- **Support**: Team members help each other succeed and overcome obstacles
- **Continuous Learning**: Commitment to learning and improving both individually and as a team

#### Communication Standards
- **Response Time**: Email within 24 hours, chat within 4 hours during business hours
- **Meeting Etiquette**: Be on time, come prepared, participate actively
- **Conflict Resolution**: Address conflicts directly and professionally, escalate when needed
- **Documentation**: Document decisions, assumptions, and key discussions

#### Work Standards
- **Quality**: Deliver work that meets agreed-upon quality standards
- **Deadlines**: Communicate early if deadlines are at risk
- **Collaboration**: Seek input and feedback before making significant decisions
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Share knowledge and document learnings for the team

### Decision-Making Process
#### Decision Authority Matrix
| Decision Type | Authority | Process | Escalation |
|---------------|-----------|---------|------------|
| Daily operational | Individual team members | Self-directed | Team lead if needed |
| Technical approach | Technical Lead | Team discussion | Project Manager |
| Scope changes | Project Manager | Formal change process | Project Sponsor |
| Resource allocation | Project Manager | Team consultation | Resource Manager |

#### Consensus Building
- **Small Decisions**: Informal discussion and agreement
- **Medium Decisions**: Structured discussion with documented outcome
- **Large Decisions**: Formal analysis, stakeholder input, documented approval

### Conflict Resolution
#### Resolution Process
1. **Direct Discussion**: Parties attempt to resolve directly
2. **Team Mediation**: Team lead facilitates discussion
3. **Management Escalation**: Project Manager or functional manager involvement
4. **Formal Process**: HR or organizational escalation if needed

#### Escalation Triggers
- Impact on project timeline or quality
- Inability to reach resolution at team level
- Recurring conflicts affecting team dynamics
- Personal or professional conduct issues
