## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Purpose
This Risk Management Plan defines how risks will be identified, analyzed, and managed throughout the project lifecycle.

### 1.2 Scope
This plan covers all project-related risks including:
- Technical risks
- Business risks
- Operational risks
- External risks
- Resource risks

### 1.3 Risk Management Objectives
- Minimize the probability and impact of negative risks
- Maximize the probability and impact of positive risks (opportunities)
- Provide systematic approach to risk management
- Enable proactive risk response

## 2. Risk Management Methodology

### 2.1 Risk Management Process
1. **Risk Identification** - Identify potential risks
2. **Risk Analysis** - Assess probability and impact
3. **Risk Response Planning** - Develop response strategies
4. **Risk Monitoring** - Track risks and responses
5. **Risk Control** - Implement risk responses

### 2.2 Risk Categories
| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Technical | Technology-related risks | System failures, integration issues |
| Business | Business environment risks | Market changes, competition |
| Operational | Day-to-day operation risks | Resource availability, process issues |
| External | Outside project control | Regulatory changes, vendor issues |
| Resource | People and equipment risks | Key person departure, equipment failure |

### 2.3 Risk Identification Techniques
- Brainstorming sessions
- Expert interviews
- Historical data analysis
- Checklist reviews
- SWOT analysis
- Root cause analysis

## 3. Risk Assessment Framework

### 3.1 Probability Scale
| Level | Description | Probability Range |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Very High | Almost certain to occur | 81-100% |
| High | Likely to occur | 61-80% |
| Medium | May occur | 41-60% |
| Low | Unlikely to occur | 21-40% |
| Very Low | Rare, but possible | 1-20% |

### 3.2 Impact Scale
| Level | Cost Impact | Schedule Impact | Quality Impact | Scope Impact |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|--------------|
| Very High | >20% increase | >20% delay | Major degradation | Major scope reduction |
| High | 10-20% increase | 10-20% delay | Significant degradation | Significant scope reduction |
| Medium | 5-10% increase | 5-10% delay | Moderate degradation | Moderate scope reduction |
| Low | 1-5% increase | 1-5% delay | Minor degradation | Minor scope reduction |
| Very Low | <1% increase | <1% delay | Negligible impact | Negligible impact |

### 3.4 Risk Priority Levels
- **Critical (20-25)**: Immediate attention required
- **High (15-19)**: Senior management attention
- **Medium (8-14)**: Management attention
- **Low (4-7)**: Monitor and manage
- **Very Low (1-3)**: Accept and monitor

## 4. Risk Response Strategies

### 4.1 Negative Risk (Threat) Responses
- **Avoid**: Eliminate the risk by changing the project plan
- **Mitigate**: Reduce probability or impact
- **Transfer**: Shift risk to third party (insurance, contracts)
- **Accept**: Acknowledge risk and take no action

### 4.2 Positive Risk (Opportunity) Responses
- **Exploit**: Ensure the opportunity is realized
- **Enhance**: Increase probability or positive impact
- **Share**: Allocate ownership to third party best able to capture opportunity
- **Accept**: Take advantage if opportunity occurs

### 4.3 Response Planning Criteria
| Risk Level | Response Required | Timeline | Authority |
|------------|-------------------|----------|-----------|
| Critical | Mandatory response plan | Immediate | Project Sponsor |
| High | Detailed response plan | Within 1 week | Project Manager |
| Medium | Basic response plan | Within 2 weeks | Risk Owner |
| Low | Monitor only | As needed | Risk Owner |
| Very Low | Document only | As needed | Risk Owner |
