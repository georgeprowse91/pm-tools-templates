# Team Charter Template

## Purpose/Overview
This team charter template establishes clear expectations, roles, and working agreements for project teams. It serves as a foundational document that aligns team members on goals, responsibilities, communication protocols, and performance standards. The charter promotes team cohesion, reduces conflicts, and provides a reference for decision-making and accountability.

## Usage Instructions
1. **Team Workshop**: Create the charter collaboratively with all team members present
2. **Leadership Review**: Involve project sponsors and stakeholders in goal-setting sections
3. **Regular Updates**: Review and revise the charter at project milestones
4. **Reference Document**: Use during team meetings to resolve conflicts and clarify expectations
5. **New Member Onboarding**: Share with new team members as part of orientation
6. **Performance Management**: Reference working agreements during performance discussions
7. **Continuous Improvement**: Update based on retrospective feedback and lessons learned
8. **Visibility**: Keep the charter accessible to all team members and stakeholders

## 4. Team Working Agreements

### 4.1 Core Working Principles
- **Collaboration**: We work together toward common goals
- **Transparency**: We communicate openly and honestly
- **Accountability**: We take ownership of our commitments
- **Quality**: We deliver high-quality work consistently
- **Continuous Improvement**: We learn and adapt regularly
- **Respect**: We value diverse perspectives and treat each other with respect

### 4.2 Communication Guidelines

#### Communication Channels
| Purpose | Channel | Frequency | Participants |
|---------|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Daily coordination | Standup meeting | Daily | Core team |
| Project updates | Email/Slack | Weekly | All stakeholders |
| Issue escalation | Direct call/Slack | As needed | Relevant parties |
| Status reporting | Team meeting | Weekly | Full team |

#### Communication Standards
- **Response Time**: Email within 24 hours, Slack within 4 hours during business hours
- **Meeting Etiquette**: Start on time, come prepared, stay engaged, end with clear actions
- **Documentation**: Record key decisions and share meeting notes within 24 hours
- **Escalation**: Use defined escalation paths for issues requiring higher-level input

### 4.3 Meeting Protocols

#### Daily Standup
- **Time**: [Day/Time]
- **Duration**: 15 minutes maximum
- **Format**: What did you do yesterday? What will you do today? Any blockers?
- **Ground Rules**: Stand up, stay focused, park detailed discussions

#### Weekly Team Meeting
- **Time**: [Day/Time]
- **Duration**: 1 hour
- **Agenda**: Progress review, upcoming work, issues and risks, team announcements
- **Preparation**: Come with status updates and agenda items

#### Retrospectives
- **Frequency**: End of each sprint/milestone
- **Duration**: 1-2 hours
- **Focus**: What went well, what could improve, action items for next period

### 4.4 Decision-Making Process

#### Decision Authority Levels
| Decision Type | Authority Level | Process |
|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| Daily work decisions | Individual team member | Self-directed |
| Technical approach | Team Lead + relevant experts | Team discussion and consensus |
| Scope changes | Project Manager | Formal change request process |
| Resource allocation | Project Manager + Sponsor | Business case and approval |

#### Consensus Building
- **Discussion**: Open dialogue to understand all perspectives
- **Options**: Identify and evaluate alternatives
- **Decision**: Seek consensus; use PM as tiebreaker if needed
- **Commitment**: All team members commit to decisions once made

## 7. Conflict Resolution

### 7.1 Conflict Resolution Process
1. **Direct Discussion**: Individuals attempt to resolve directly
2. **Team Lead Mediation**: Team lead facilitates discussion if needed
3. **Project Manager Involvement**: PM helps resolve if team lead unsuccessful
4. **Escalation**: Involve functional managers or sponsors if necessary

### 7.2 Ground Rules for Conflict Resolution
- **Focus on Issues**: Address the problem, not the person
- **Listen Actively**: Understand all perspectives before responding
- **Seek Solutions**: Focus on finding mutually acceptable solutions
- **Learn and Improve**: Use conflicts as opportunities to improve processes

## 8. Recognition and Rewards

### 8.1 Recognition Principles
- **Timely**: Recognize achievements close to when they occur
- **Specific**: Be clear about what is being recognized
- **Public**: Share recognition with the broader team when appropriate
- **Varied**: Use different forms of recognition for different people

### 8.2 Recognition Methods
- **Peer Recognition**: Team members nominate each other for contributions
- **Management Recognition**: Formal recognition from project leadership
- **Public Recognition**: Sharing achievements in team meetings or newsletters
- **Professional Development**: Opportunities for growth and new responsibilities
