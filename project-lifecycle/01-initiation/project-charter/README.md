## üéØ Methodology-Specific Guidance

### PMBOK/Traditional Approach
**Focus: Comprehensive Definition & Authority**

Key Elements:
- **Project Purpose/Justification** - Why this project exists
- **High-Level Requirements** - What will be delivered
- **Success Criteria** - How success will be measured
- **Constraints & Assumptions** - Project boundaries
- **Project Manager Authority** - Your decision-making power

**When to Use:**
- Regulatory or compliance-driven projects
- Large, complex initiatives with clear scope
- Projects requiring detailed upfront planning

### Agile Approach
**Focus: Vision & Team Alignment**

Key Elements:
- **Product Vision** - The future state you're building toward
- **Success Metrics** - Key performance indicators
- **Team Working Agreements** - How the team will collaborate
- **Definition of Done** - Quality standards
- **Stakeholder Engagement Plan** - Communication approach

**When to Use:**
- Software development projects
- Innovation initiatives with evolving requirements
- Small to medium cross-functional teams

### Hybrid Approach
**Focus: Adaptive Structure**

Key Elements:
- **Predictable Components** - Traditional charter elements for stable areas
- **Adaptive Components** - Agile elements for uncertain areas
- **Governance Framework** - How decisions will be made across approaches
- **Integration Points** - Where methodologies intersect
- **Success Criteria** - Metrics for both predictable and adaptive work

**When to Use:**
- Digital transformation projects
- Large programs with mixed methodology needs
- Organizations transitioning between methodologies

## ‚úÖ Approval Process

### 1. Draft Creation (Week 1)
- Use appropriate template for your methodology
- Collaborate with key stakeholders on content
- Focus on clarity and alignment

### 2. Stakeholder Review (Week 1-2)
- Circulate draft to sponsor and key stakeholders
- Gather feedback and incorporate changes
- Ensure business alignment

### 3. Formal Approval (Week 2)
- Present to project sponsor/steering committee
- Obtain written approval/signatures
- Communicate approval to organization

### 4. Baseline & Communication (Week 2)
- Store approved charter in project repository
- Communicate charter to all stakeholders
- Use as reference for scope and decision-making

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Too Much Detail Too Early
‚ùå **Avoid:** Writing a 20-page charter with detailed requirements
‚úÖ **Instead:** Keep it high-level, use charter to authorize further planning

### Missing Success Criteria
‚ùå **Avoid:** Vague statements like "improve customer satisfaction"
‚úÖ **Instead:** Specific, measurable criteria like "increase NPS by 15 points"

### Unclear Authority
‚ùå **Avoid:** Generic PM authority statements
‚úÖ **Instead:** Specific decision-making power and resource access

### No Stakeholder Engagement
‚ùå **Avoid:** Writing charter in isolation
‚úÖ **Instead:** Collaborate with sponsor and key stakeholders throughout

### Wrong Template for Project Type
‚ùå **Avoid:** Using traditional charter for Agile project
‚úÖ **Instead:** Match template to methodology and project characteristics

## üìä Charter Quality Checklist

### Content Quality
- [ ] Purpose is clear and compelling
- [ ] Success criteria are specific and measurable
- [ ] Key stakeholders are identified
- [ ] Timeline and budget are realistic
- [ ] Risks and constraints are acknowledged

### Process Quality
- [ ] Stakeholder input was gathered
- [ ] Sponsor has reviewed and approved
- [ ] Charter is stored in accessible location
- [ ] Team has been briefed on charter content
- [ ] Charter will be referenced in project decisions

## üí° Pro Tips

### For First-Time Project Managers
1. **Start with the template** that matches your methodology
2. **Get stakeholder input early** - don't write in isolation
3. **Keep it simple** - charter authorizes, doesn't detail everything

### For Experienced Project Managers
1. **Customize templates** to your organization's needs
2. **Use charter throughout project** as decision-making reference
3. **Template hybrid approaches** for complex organizational needs

### For Organizations
1. **Standardize charter templates** across the organization
2. **Train sponsors** on their role in charter approval
3. **Link charters to portfolio management** for strategic alignment
